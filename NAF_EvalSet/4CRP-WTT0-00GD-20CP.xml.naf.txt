The investment will be supported with a EUR 9.5 million government grant . The aid was granted by the economy ministry on an individual basis , a form of support that is available for large firms who plan to carry out investments exceeding EUR 50 million . This new , EU-compatible support form was first applied to electronics manufacturer Bosch , which signed a EUR 15 million support agreement last week . Construction of Denso 's new facilities will be completed at the end of August . The company will manufacture diesel fuel pumps that will be used in Ford and Fiat engines . The new factories will employ 1100 workers , taking the number of workers at Denso Hungary to 2500 by the end of 2005 . As a result of the investment , Denso Hungary 's sales revenues are expected to rise from EUR 133 million last year to EUR 320 million by 2006 . Currently , the company 's major clients are Toyota , Isuzu , Nissan , Volkswagen and Audi , but the Hungarian subsidiary also delivers components used in Land Rover , Ford , Jaguar , Peugeot and Lamborghini cars . The output of Hungary 's automotive industry totaled USD 8.46 billion in 2003 , and accounted for 15 % of all manufacturing in Hungary , according to the ministry 's figures . Within this , automotive component manufacturers recorded sales approaching USD 1.5 billion . In all , 13 of the world 's 30 largest automotive suppliers are present in Hungary . Of cars assembled in Hungary , 94.3 % are exported , while exports account for 87.6 % of the automotive components industry , according to the ministry .]]></raw>
