According to Reuters , Berlin will have two months to respond before the EU can formally take the issue back to the European court . Germany rewrote the VW law when the European court of justice ruled that original law , dating back to the 1959 , contravened European union rules on the free flow of capital . But Germany retained a clause that means the state of Lower Saxony can effectively block a hostile takeover of VW through a 20 % stake that it holds and this is being contested . Normally , under German company law , such a right of veto only applies to organisations with a 25 % shareholding . Porsche is the largest shareholder in Volkswagen with the equivalent of a 74.1 % stake . It has said it wants to raise this to over 75 % and is keen to see the VW law abolished so that it can exert full control over the company .]]></raw>
