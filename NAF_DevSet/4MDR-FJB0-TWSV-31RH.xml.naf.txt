  <raw><![CDATA[ Shoppers ignore hybrids in China BEIJING -- You 'll be able to buy a General Motors hybrid in China in 2008 , says Kevin Wale , president of the General Motors China Group . The question is : Will anyone want to buy a hybrid ? Usually , an automaker assembles models it believes the market wants . But in China , where government relations are an important business tool , automakers are hoping hybrids will do the trick . China wants to clean up its air and reduce oil consumption . So the powerful central government included a clause encouraging hybrid technology in its July 2004 automotive policy . Hybrids use internal combustion engines and one or more electric motors to drive the wheels . Toyota Motor Corp. was the first foreign automaker to assemble a hybrid - the Prius - in China . Sales are slow , but Toyota never expected it to be a big seller , said Yoshimi Inaba , executive vice president of Toyota Motor . Inaba and Wale spoke at the Automotive News China Conference here last week . Local automaker SAIC Motor Corp. is working on its own hybrid model because the Shanghai government asked it to , says a former SAIC manager . Chery Automobile Co. also plans to offer a hybrid model . The most important thing Chinese consumers seek when car shopping is value for money , which includes gadgets and a roomy interior . Few shoppers will spend money to help the environment . Market forecaster CSM Worldwide reckons the market for hybrids in China by 2012 will be a scant 10,000 units . China 's cities have some of the world 's dirtiest air . So fuel-efficient hybrids will help clean the air . If the government passes policies such as tax breaks to encourage hybrid sales , demand could increase . But , so far , hybrids in China are being driven by government policy , not consumers .]]></raw>
