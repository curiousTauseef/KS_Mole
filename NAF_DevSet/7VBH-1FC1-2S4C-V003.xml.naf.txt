  <raw><![CDATA[ US drug sales growth slows to 1.3 % Annual US prescription sales growth was just 1.3 % in 2008 , reaching $ 291.5 billion , according to IMS Health , a leading provider of market intelligence to the pharmaceutical and health care industries . Dispensed prescription volume in the USA grew at a 0.9 % pace , it added . Factors influencing the market 's slower growth in 2008 included higher demand for less-expensive generic medicines , lower new product sales and reduced consumer demand due to the economic turndown . Across the major therapeutic classes , the biggest sales came from antipsychotics , increasing 11.5 % to $ 14.6 billion . Lipid regulators , or cholesterol-lowerers , were the most widely-dispensed retail prescription medications on a volume basis in 2008 , but second by value reaching a total of $ 14.6 billion , down 11.6 % on 2007 . This was followed by proton pump inhibitors at $ 13.9 billion , seeing a decline of 2.1 % , and drugs for seizure disorders , totaling $ 1.3 billion , a rise of 10.8 % . In terms of individual drugs , Pfizer 's mega block buster statin Lipitor ( atorvastatin ) generated the highest turnover of $ 7.8 billion , but down year-on-year by 3.7 % . Next were : AstraZeneca 's PPI Nexium ( esomeprazole ) , at $ 5.9 billion and 7.3 % higher than in 2007 ; and Sanofi-Aventis and Bristol-Myers Squibb 's blood thinner Plavix , at $ 4.9 billion and leaping 26.4 % . Biotechnology-based medicines accounted for 15 % , or $ 45.9 billion , of the total US market , IMS said . Cost sensitivity on part of consumers `` It is the lowest growth we have ever seen , '' said Diana Conmy , corporate director of market insights for IMS . `` Patients are going to try to hang on to their prescription medicines as much as they can , but in some cases they have to make decisions on taking on a new therapy or moving from a branded to a generic and there is definitely a cost sensitivity , '' she added . Ms Conmy also noted that the slowdown in demand has been worse during this current recession than those in the past because the cost of insurance premiums and co-payments have risen , putting more pressure on patients .]]></raw>
