  <raw><![CDATA[ Town set to benefit CHORLEY MP Lindsay Hoyle co-hosted the Westminster launch of a new partnership designed to improve the productivity of North West aerospace companies . The collaboration between the North West Aerospace Alliance ( NWAA ) and the Society of British Aerospace Companies ( SBAC ) should greatly benefit local companies in Chorley as well as the North West . The aims of the link are to raise skill levels , provide a catalyst for innovation and improve industry supply chains , all key in the challenging global market place . Mr Hoyle 's launch speech revolved around the need to match the skills young people acquire at college with the requirements of the world beating aerospace companies in the North West . He said : `` Work-based degrees and qualifications can help to ensure the needs of industry are met by universities . '' The aerospace industry is a significant employer in the North West supporting more than 60,000 jobs . It also produces around ? 6.8 billion in aerospace output each year . SBAC president , Kevin Smith , said : `` I am delighted that the North West Aerospace Alliance has become the SBAC 's Regional Partner . '' The partnership was announced to MPs at a special reception in the Atlee Suite , in the House of Commons . Representatives of leading aerospace companies attended the presentation , those present including Airbus , BAE Systems , Rolls-Royce plc , Bombardier and Thales UK .]]></raw>
