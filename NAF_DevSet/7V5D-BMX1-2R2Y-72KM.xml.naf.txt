  <raw><![CDATA[ Toyota-GM joint venture in California to implement furlough plan Mar. 6 -- NEW YORK -- United Auto Workers members at a joint venture between Toyota Motor Corp. and General Motors Corp. in California have approved a furlough proposal from management , Toyota officials have said . The union members at New United Motor Manufacturing Inc. in Fremont , California voted 86.5 percent in support of the proposal , which requires them to take Fridays off without pay , the officials said . The furlough , effective from Friday through Aug. 7 , is part of a work-sharing program Toyota earlier decided to implement at some North American factories to protect jobs amid production cuts due to dwindling sales . The factory was an old GM one that first opened in the early 1960s . It reopened in 1984 as a joint venture of the two carmakers . Toyota 's new car sales in the United States fell nearly 40 percent in February from a year earlier while GM recorded a 53 percent year-to-year fall in the same month .
